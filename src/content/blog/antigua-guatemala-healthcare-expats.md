## Healthcare in Antigua Guatemala Is More Accessible Than You Think

One of the biggest concerns for anyone considering a move abroad is healthcare. In Antigua Guatemala, expats consistently find that medical care is surprisingly affordable, accessible, and — in many private facilities — comparable to what you'd find in the United States or Europe. Whether you need a routine checkup, dental work, or emergency care, Antigua and nearby Guatemala City have you covered.

## How Healthcare Works in Guatemala

Guatemala has a two-tier healthcare system: public and private. The public system (administered through the Ministry of Public Health and Social Assistance) provides free or low-cost care to residents, but it's often overcrowded and underfunded. Most expats and retirees in Antigua opt for private healthcare, which offers shorter wait times, English-speaking doctors, and modern facilities.

The good news? Private healthcare in Guatemala costs a fraction of what you'd pay in North America or Europe. A specialist consultation typically runs between Q250–Q500 ($33–$66 USD), and many procedures that cost thousands of dollars in the US are available for hundreds here.

## Hospitals and Clinics Near Antigua Guatemala

### Hospital Nacional de Antigua
Located right in Antigua, this is the go-to facility for most expats. It offers 24-hour emergency care, imaging, laboratory services, surgery, and specialist consultations. The staff is accustomed to treating international patients, and many doctors speak English.

### Privately Owned Clinics in Antigua
Several smaller clinics and family practices operate within the city center. These are ideal for routine visits, minor injuries, prescriptions, and follow-up care. Walk-in availability is common, and you rarely need an appointment more than a day or two in advance.

### Guatemala City Hospitals (45 Minutes Away)
For advanced procedures, complex diagnostics, or specialized surgery, Guatemala City is just a 45-minute drive from Antigua. Facilities like Hospital Centro Médico, Hospital Herrera Llerandi, and Hospital Universitario offer world-class care with state-of-the-art equipment. Many expats make the trip for annual checkups or specific specialist needs.

## Dental Care: A Major Perk

Dental care in Guatemala deserves special mention. High-quality dental work — including crowns, implants, root canals, and cosmetic dentistry — is available at 50–70% less than US prices. Many dentists in Antigua and Guatemala City trained in the US or Europe and use the same materials and equipment.

A routine cleaning costs around Q150–Q300 ($20–$40 USD), and a porcelain crown runs about Q2,000–Q3,500 ($260–$460 USD) — compared to $1,000–$2,000 in the States. Some expats even plan dental tourism trips, combining their appointment with a weekend exploring Antigua's cobblestone streets.

## Health Insurance Options for Expats

### Local Guatemalan Insurance
Several Guatemalan insurers offer health plans to foreign residents. Companies like AGI, El Roble, and Mapfre provide policies that cover hospitalization, surgery, outpatient visits, and prescription medications. Monthly premiums for a healthy individual typically range from Q500–Q1,500 ($65–$200 USD), depending on age and coverage level.

### International Health Insurance
If you want global coverage that works both in Guatemala and when traveling home, international providers like Cigna Global, Allianz Care, and SafetyWing offer plans tailored to expats. These tend to cost more but provide the peace of mind of coverage wherever you go.

### Paying Out of Pocket
Many expats — especially those from the US accustomed to high deductibles — choose to pay out of pocket for routine care and carry catastrophic-only insurance. Given the low cost of medical services in Guatemala, this approach can make financial sense for healthy individuals. A full year of doctor visits, dental cleanings, and minor procedures might total $500–$1,000 USD.

## Pharmacies and Prescriptions

Pharmacies (*farmacias*) are everywhere in Antigua, and many medications that require a prescription in the US are available over the counter in Guatemala — including antibiotics, blood pressure medication, and allergy treatments. Farmacia Galeno and Farmacia Batres are reliable chains with knowledgeable pharmacists.

Common medications cost significantly less. A course of antibiotics that might cost $50–$100 in the US without insurance can be purchased for Q30–Q80 ($4–$11 USD) in Antigua. For expats managing chronic conditions, the savings on monthly prescriptions alone can be substantial.

## Emergency Services

Antigua has ambulance services, though response times can vary. For critical emergencies, the Hospital Nacional de Antigua provides 24/7 emergency care. Many expats keep the contact information for private ambulance services and their nearest hospital saved on their phones.

Guatemala's emergency number is **110** for police and **123** for firefighters. For medical emergencies, calling the hospital directly is often faster than waiting for a dispatched ambulance.

## Tips for Managing Healthcare as an Expat

- **Bring your medical records.** Have your records translated into Spanish before your move. This saves time when establishing care with new providers.
- **Build relationships early.** Don't wait for an emergency to find a doctor. Schedule a wellness visit shortly after arriving to establish yourself as a patient.
- **Keep a medication list.** Active ingredients may go by different names in Guatemala. Know the generic names of your prescriptions.
- **Consider a medical evacuation plan.** For rare but serious emergencies, services like Global Rescue or Medjet can arrange transport to a facility of your choice.
- **Ask your neighbors.** The expat community in Antigua is tight-knit and generous with recommendations for trusted doctors, dentists, and specialists.
