Finding a good real estate agent in Antigua Guatemala can feel overwhelming, especially if you are new to the area or buying property in Guatemala for the first time. The market here works differently than in North America or Europe, and having the right person on your side makes a huge difference.

## Why Local Expertise Matters

Antigua Guatemala has a unique real estate market. Property values vary dramatically from one block to the next, zoning rules are loosely enforced, and title issues are more common than you might expect. An agent who works this market every day knows which neighborhoods are rising, which developers deliver on time, and which properties have hidden problems.

A general real estate license in Guatemala does not require the same training or testing as in other countries. That means experience and track record matter more than credentials. Look for an agent who has been active in Antigua Guatemala for several years, not someone who just moved into real estate from another field.

## What to Look For

**Bilingual communication:** If your Spanish is limited, you need an agent who speaks fluent English. Miscommunications about contract terms, property conditions, or legal requirements can be costly. A bilingual agent bridges the gap between you and local sellers, attorneys, and notaries.

**Deep local knowledge:** Ask about specific neighborhoods, recent sales, and market trends. A good agent can tell you why one street is worth more than another, or why a property has been sitting on the market for months.

**References from past clients:** Any trustworthy agent should be able to connect you with previous buyers, especially other foreigners who have purchased through them. If they cannot or will not provide references, that is a red flag.

**Transparency about fees:** In Guatemala, real estate commissions are typically 5% of the sale price, paid by the seller. The buyer usually does not pay a commission. If an agent asks you to pay a fee upfront or on top of the purchase price, ask why and get it in writing.

**Network of professionals:** A good agent has relationships with local attorneys, notaries, surveyors, and contractors. These connections are valuable when you need a title search, property survey, or renovation estimate.

## Red Flags to Watch For

- **Pressure to buy quickly.** A trustworthy agent gives you time and space to make decisions. High-pressure tactics usually mean they care more about their commission than your interests.
- **Discouraging legal review.** If an agent tells you that you do not need a lawyer to buy property, walk away. Always have independent legal representation.
- **Vague or inconsistent information.** If the agent cannot clearly explain the property's legal status, boundaries, or history, they may not know the property well enough.
- **No written agreements.** Everything about the relationship, fees, and property terms should be documented.

## Questions to Ask a Potential Agent

Before committing to work with someone, ask these questions:

1. How long have you been working in Antigua Guatemala real estate?
2. Can you share references from recent foreign buyers?
3. How do you handle title verification and due diligence?
4. What happens if a deal falls through?
5. Do you work with a specific attorney or notary?

Their answers will tell you a lot about their professionalism and experience.
