## Why Antigua Guatemala?

Antigua Guatemala has become one of the most popular destinations for expats in Central America. With its spring-like weather, affordable cost of living, vibrant community, and stunning colonial architecture, it's easy to see why.

## Visa Requirements

Most nationalities can enter Guatemala visa-free for up to 90 days. After that, you can extend for another 90 days at the immigration office. For longer stays, you'll need to apply for temporary residency.

## Finding Your First Home

The rental market in Antigua offers something for every budget:
- **Budget**: $300-500/month for a basic apartment
- **Mid-range**: $500-1,000/month for a furnished colonial home
- **Premium**: $1,000-2,500/month for a luxury property

## Cost of Living

Antigua offers an incredibly affordable lifestyle:
- **Rent**: $300-1,500/month
- **Groceries**: $150-300/month
- **Dining out**: $5-15 per meal
- **Transportation**: $1-3 per ride
- **Healthcare**: $30-50 per doctor visit

## Getting Around

Antigua is a walkable city — most things are within a 15-minute walk. For longer trips, tuk-tuks cost $1-2 and Uber is available for trips to Guatemala City.
